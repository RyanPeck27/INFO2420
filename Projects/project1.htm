Web Site Critiques

Utah National Parks – http://www.utah.com/nationalparks/

  1. First Impression: The site has a clean and professional look. The imagery is stunning and immediately makes you want to explore Utah’s parks.
  2. Information Flow: The layout uses a top navigation bar with dropdown menus, along with visual links in the main content area. There's a clear focus on helping users find information quickly.
  3. Structure: The structure is logical. It’s divided by park names and travel categories (things to do, places to stay, maps, etc.). It’s very intuitive for users planning trips.
  4. Graphics/Layout: High-quality photos and a well-balanced layout greatly enhance the site. The imagery adds value and effectively showcases the beauty of Utah’s parks.
  5. Load Time: The homepage loaded in about 3 seconds. Considering the large images, that’s a reasonable load time.
  6. Text Readability: The text is clear and easy to read. Font size and contrast are appropriate. Background colors are subtle and do not interfere with readability.
  7. Navigation: The site uses both a top navigation bar and in-page links. It’s easy to move between sections and find what you’re looking for. Navigation is very well done.
  8. Look and Feel Consistency: Yes, the visual style and layout remain consistent across all pages. The design reinforces the site's theme of natural exploration and travel.
  9. Responsive Design: The site adjusts nicely to smaller screen sizes. Navigation collapses into a mobile-friendly menu, and content stacks vertically. It's well-optimized for mobile use.
  10. Unusual or Well Done: The interactive maps and itinerary suggestions are particularly helpful and well-executed. It also integrates well with other travel planning tools and local recommendations.
